## Drum tum 肚子鼓

### tum 这词只是为了压韵吗？我怎么不认识？

原来是肚子，孩子把 stomach 拼成 tummy，又简化成 tum。

> tum or tum·my| ˈtəmē | noun (plural tummies) *informal* 
>
> a person's stomach or abdomen: *[as modifier]* *: a tummy upset*. 
>
> ORIGIN mid 19th century: child's pronunciation of [stomach](x-dictionary:r:m_en_gbus0997650:com.apple.dictionary.NOAD:stomach).
>

### “Dum dum diddle” 又是什么鬼？

这是一首上世纪七十年代的美国民谣歌曲名，两个女生自编自唱，讲对一位忧郁、敏感的男生的爱慕。词挺傻，但歌很火。参考维基页。

副歌唱到：

> Dum dum diddle to be your fiddle, 
>
> To be so near ya and not just hear ya
>
> Dum dum diddle, to be your fiddle,
>
> I think then maybe youd see me, baby
>
> You'd be mine
>
> And we'd be together all the time
>
> Wish I was dum dum diddle, your darling fiddle.

“fiddle”是小提琴的别名，当它用于民谣，而不用作经典曲目时，常被叫作“fiddle”。

> fid·dle| ˈfidl | noun 1 *informal* a violin, especially when used to play folk music.
>
> fit as a fiddle (also as fit as a fiddle) in very good health.

### “Chant”与“speak, read, say”有什么区别？

“Chant”与“speak, read, say”根本不是一回事，它与“sing, shout, intone”类似，都要带节奏。

> 1. To [sing](kiwix://e5071ef0-6832-c903-7090-e9810cd621fd/A/sing), especially without instruments, and as applied to [monophonic](kiwix://e5071ef0-6832-c903-7090-e9810cd621fd/A/monophonic) and pre-modern music.

### “Verse”是多长的段落？

诗多用反复的结构，“Verse”就是其中相同结构的一个段落。

> 1. One of several similar units of a song, consisting of several lines, generally rhymed.
